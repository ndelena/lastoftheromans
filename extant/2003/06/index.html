Extant: June 2003 Archives

late night counterinsurgency battles

By
Nick DeLena
on June 28, 2003 12:00 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i returned home last night from the fnx best music poll at the fleet boston pavilion around 11:30 last night. i was pretty tired from the heat, so i sat down to work on finishing my new website. after about an hour and a half of designing, i decided to go to bed. so after i get undressed, i decide to get a glass of water.

lo and behold...

as i enter the kitchen, i spot a cockroach. the same limey bastard who evaded me the previous night.

aside: alright. i don't like insects. spiders are bad and those centerpede-like wallclinging 'revere bugs' are bad enough. but it doesn't get worse than cockroaches.

i went into attack mode. the little bugger was out of range; he was in between my stove and the wall divider (that separates my little coffee nook from the stove). so i couldn't rain fatal blows upon him with my skillet. i reached to my right for the first object - a bottle of ammonia. i figured that would provoke him out of his spot.

in my haste, i just opened the bottle and sort of 'flung' ammonia at it; which surely provoked it, as it ran out and i utterly flattened it, instantly killing it. but my struggle was not yet over.

i had ammonia all over the floor. and it was the hyper-concentrated kind.

i grabbed my mop from the bathroom, ran back to the kitchen and stepped in and started mopping.

instantly my eyes started burning from the ammonia, i couldn't keep them open it hurt so bad. i soon couldn't breathe.

so i ran back to my room, and grabbed a shirt to put over my nose. i had to dilute the ammonia with water first then try and mop it up, all with one hand (as the other hand held the shirt over my nose so i could breathe) and all in just my boxers.

::sigh::

atypical historical thought

By
Nick DeLena
on June 23, 2003 3:27 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

last night i dreamed that we had a high school reunion. although it is entirely irrespective of history, people appeared glad that i was there. at one point we all became children again; we appeared as we were in elementary school, small children, but retained the same intellect, maybe to analyze what it was like being a child firsthand.

i've been having these symbolically complex dreams lately, which is atypical; my dreams are usually about extraordinarily mundane things (e.g. laundry, walking to work, talking with friends). it would be easy to say that this nascent change is the product of me reanalyzing my future. but perhaps this specific dream is attempting to resolve the old cliche of whether one would be happier as a child or as an adult.

i believed i am happier now and in spite of the dream and all its temptations of a burden-less felicity i continue to believe that now is where i would prefer to be.

my mother made these awesome tables for my apartment. one coffee table and two end tables. they are black with white tiled tops. they match our black leather couches and our french posters. our living room is really turning film noir. it's impressive.

i took some pictures and as soon as i can get my server back i'll post them.

no kaiser, no know-how

By
Nick DeLena
on June 5, 2003 1:47 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i've been getting used to the rigors of beacon hill lately; referring to the back bay as the suburbs, chatting with the neighbors about equity and dividend policy, and professing my appreciation for a 1990 chateau du basquia bordeaux.

actually, i haven't done any of that. i've been sitting on my ass, drinking beer and playing guitar in my bedroom.

the apartment is interesting- it's the first first-floor apartment i've ever lived in, so my bedroom window is slightly higher than street level, but i can see the heads of taller people as they walk by. there have been a few occasions when, as i lift my head up after playing some radiohead, i see a few people looking in the window. they are typically immediately embarassed and walk away. it is half disturbing half comforting.

i've already worked 36 hours this week, and it is only 2pm on thursday. still have another afternoon and a full day ahead. with overtime, i'll have made enough money this week to pay my month's rent and utilities. that is my only consolation.

perhaps tonight i'll take some pictures of my apartment.
