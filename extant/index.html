Another

By
Nick DeLena
on November 12, 2004 5:07 PM | Permalink | Comments (4) | TrackBacks (0)

Much of southern New England is currently getting the season's first real taste of snow with accumulations of up to five inches expected before the storm moves out of the region on Saturday. The National Weather Service at Taunton in an advisory issued shortly after noon said the heaviest snowfall would come after midnight into Saturday morning. The areas expected to be hardest hit are interior southern Rhode Island and south coastal Massachusetts which includes New Bedford and Fall River. The forecast calls for between three and five inches of snow in those areas. The early stages of the storm has seen a mixture of snow and rain across most of southern New England which also includes northern Rhode Island and northern Connecticut. The NWS advisory said temperatures would drop steadily during the night with lows ranging from 30 to 35 in the Boston area while well inland the readings would be between 25 and 30 degrees.

I took the above picture about a week ago, through my living room window. Currently - just one week later - the tree is nearly leafless, covered in wet slush, what leaves are left are frozen and shriveled. Welcome to another New England winter.

¿Te gusta conducír?

By
Nick DeLena
on June 27, 2004 4:07 PM | Permalink | Comments (3) | TrackBacks (0)

As many of you know, I purchased a 1994 Volkswagen Jetta back in March. Now the car was a salvage; it had previously been in an accident and deemed a "total loss." I rebuilt the car and am now attempting to get it registered. But the Massachusetts bureacratic process apparently has other plans in mind. The first step is to get the car inspected by the state police. The car has twice failed the process: once because I drove the car there (you can't do this, but they don't tell you beforehand), and just today the car failed because airbag light never came on. An important safety feature of the car, no doubt, but this is not the Massachusetts safety inspection, it's a fraud/theft inspection to verify that you have indeed rebuilt the car with legitimate parts. The safety inspection is a separate process.

Either way, the car failed again and now I have to get the airbag fixed and certified by a VW dealer, so as we speak it's sitting at Boston Volkswagen in Allston. Remind me never to deal with salvage titles again.

Un mare di risate.

By
Nick DeLena
on April 19, 2004 12:50 AM | Permalink | Comments (2) | TrackBacks (0)

Back in September I posted about an Ellis Island record I found regarding my paternal great grandfather's immigration to the U.S. and my ongoing attempts to acquire dual Italian-U.S. citizenship. The record I initially found turned out to be of my great-great uncle Gennara, who in turn settled in Mount Vernon, New York and started a New York/New Jersey branch of the DeLenas (DiLenas).

My true great grandfather's record was harder to find, thanks to t
he slow-witted Ellis Island employees who screwed up the spelling of my great grandfather's home town of Afragola, spelling it "Apagola." His information is as follows:

Name: DiLena, Giuseppe
Ethnicity: Italian
Place of Residence: Apagola, Italy (should read Afragola)
Date of Arrival: May 17, 1907
Age on Arrival: 23
Gender: Male
Marital Status: Single
Ship of travel: Madonna
Port of Departure: Naples, Campania, Italy

Interestingly, after I posted the original September 25th, 2003 piece, someone from Afragola came across it (I'm not quite sure how, as I am not a prominent result on any google searches for Italian ancestry related to Naples, Campania or Afragola). On this message board, the man posted (in Italian), regarding what he read on my site as well as the comments between me and Chris that followed the original post.
