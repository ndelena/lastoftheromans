Accounting the bandit.

By
Nick DeLena
on August 23, 2002 9:10 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

yesterday: haircut, picked alison up, chilled, had an exhaustive managerial accounting final, went to tt the bears and saw 'the burning paris' which was a worthwhile investment. the managerial accounting final i had was a war of attrition. the exam was comprised of five main 'problems' which were then divided into five questions. each problem involved a different company with certain transactions or financial misreportings. i performed solidly on job order costing, absorption/variable costing, cost-volume-profit stuff, overhead allocation, product costs and such, but i think my aircraft began to break apart at the extensive section on the merchandiser budgeting process. oh well. today is friday and i have another exam at 5pm, for management science. i expect it shall be quite easy. my linux install was halted at the video driver configuration stage. i don't know what kind of video card is in this stupid compaq box. i have not opened it yet. i guess i'll have to. that's it.

Singsong workforce blues

By
Nick DeLena
on August 21, 2002 9:07 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

The most eventful activites of late have been centered on testing the website I run at Fidelity Investments through a Solaris upgrade from 2.6 to 8. Now there are some changes coming through, which are being outsourced to Boston design firm Mediumbold. That should be fun. I was just told, however, that one of the large changes is coming through September 2nd, which means I have to be available to push that through to our dev & qa environments. But this is the day after I move into my new apartment, and the same day I had my cable modem scheduled to be installed. It looks like I'll have to get a September 1 install. Then there's the whole situation with the 'other' job at Macrosoft Associates. The original plan was to work 20 hours a week in the fall while taking classes. We have yet to agree on an hourly wage, and it has been two weeks. I'm getting skeptical. "Yo, yo. This is Skeptikal keepin it real." Anyway. At least I'm getting paid, a little bit. I need to start my own business.
