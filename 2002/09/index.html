janitors protest while i crosstrain

By
Nick DeLena
on September 30, 2002 11:53 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i went to my new gym in copley square today for the first time. it's fairly posh. all the exercise machines have built in tv screens with cd/dvd players and cable channels.

while i was there the Service Employees International Union Local 254 was protesting outside, up and down boylston street regarding the current boston janitor strike.

i watched them from the cybex elliptical crosstrainer on the third floor of the gym.

they are protesting because they get paid so little that they often have to go to work ill, lest their families go without food.

this world is full of contrasts.

a brief note from the makers of product

By
Nick DeLena
on September 25, 2002 4:00 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

the Fed does not control interest rates, merely influences them. ok.

the banking system's deposit-creating potential can be estimated using the deposit multiplier... or the coefficient of deposit expansion.... okay....

so the Fed can create money by introducing cash in the form of excess reserves (through the forced purchasing of member bank's T Bills). these excess reserves are then loaned out... and the amount that can be created can be found by using the following simple formula:

transaction deposit multiplier X excess reserves = max volume of new deposits and loans.

yeah, big test on that stuff next week. great.
