conversations with myself

By
Nick DeLena
on January 15, 2003 10:45 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

today i had my long overcoat on, my cold grey overcoat. i think i looked very professorly. my hair was windblown, i was a bit tired and disheveled. i talked about project management for too long at the journal office. i was saying things like "functional requirements" and "systems integration".

we went to cafe podima and i got a very excellent cafe au lait.

my practical financial planning class is taught by a professor from revere. he must be the first in the history of that deity-forsaken encampment. he used a word that i've only heard used by people from revere- when referring to a lump payment, like a mortgage payment or something similar, and he referred to the percentage paid to interest as a "nut". e.g. "a 20% interest rate really adds that nut to your payment". my father will use this word as well.

how peculiar. he reminds me of every late-middle-age man from revere. yet he is a professor with a doctorate in finance. education and revere just seem like two incompatible concepts to me.

there is this really great band called "third eye foundation" and they have this song called "four damaged lemons". i recommend this to all.

i'm so tired. i feel like going to sleep right now, but it's not even 11pm. and i have homework due at 11:30 tomorrow morning. should i get up early and do it then?

i do believe so yes.

it's cold and i enjoy my ephemeral sleep too much.

on the horizon: seattle in march, cuba in may(?), prague in july(?) i can attain these if i work toward them.

set goals. set realistic goals nicholas. act on them. work toward them. it's your life.

the real mission hill new years death song

By
Nick DeLena
on January 1, 2003 2:25 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

gunshots. dead people. bums. alcoholics. drug dealers. world wars. anti-americanism. terror.

new years. happy 2003 for what it means.

despite the auld lang syne tendencies of new year's i don't believe i have ever felt so truly alone, be it emotionally, intellectually, politically or philosophically. and it's very cold. and depreciating.

i'm beginning to think i've isolated myself so much... perhaps as a defensive mechanism... that i'll never truly be able to open myself to people.

and i've resigned myself to this. as my father and his father have done the same. maybe in time i'll be able to change this course. but as for right now... welcome to the cold, steel, binary future. it is thankless, frigid, isolated, and emotionless. oh smiles.
