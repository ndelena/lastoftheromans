Vintage: March 2003 Archives

Spring Ale slide

By
Nick DeLena
on March 27, 2003 1:42 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

today we floated downstream from campus and wound up sitting outside at the uno's in fanueil hall. as i was sipping my sam adams' spring ale and the warm breeze blew, i really felt like the bustling moving running life of spring is just seconds away. we came back and caught a suffolk coffeehouse performance of monologues put on by the women's center. then it was time to hit the middle east to see pinback.

PINBACK ROCKS LIKE FEW OTHER BANDS CAN ROCK.

i am tired, but there is light on the horizon.


i will say
springtime won't work so well anyway
and with december on my breath
you can say you knew me well
though my hands are lonely by themselves

St. 1625's day

By
Nick DeLena
on March 17, 2003 10:26 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

last night 1625 played host to another party. it was mostly low-key, a lot of people didn't show, or just stopped by for a few. i think throwing a party at the beginning of spring break might have been a bad idea, most people were not in the city. but i regret nothing, a good time was had. the day started with me picking up mike and driving to lowell to get
jim. we bought alcohol in nashua, new hampshire, then came back to boston. we started drinking hours before people showed up. there was one point where we turned around and jim was housed. wasted. we ordered chinese food from nan ling (blech) and devoured it.

we are so primordial when drunk.

i was hoping that several other people would show, but they made-not an appearance. i was specifically hoping alex would show, but she had a long day and crashed early. i did speak with her for a while on the phone. i am coming to realize i should have taken mike's advice long ago.

this week: spring break. i'm working. the whole week. how bogus. this is no comparison to last year's spring break in greece at the foot of mount olympus. memories...

The chapeau

By
Nick DeLena
on March 14, 2003 11:45 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

last night was one for the memoirs. it was a rowdy night at the Hat. tom ended up coming by and experiencing the magic that is the red hat on a thursday night. he arrived around midnight, when i was pretty housed, he came in and i gave him a headlock and wrestled with him for a while. it was uncharacterisic to say the least.

most people were represented there. i expected a large amount of the conversation to focus on the sga anti-war resolution, but it was a pretty un-serious night. i spoke to some people that i haven't seen in a while and i realized that i had been an idiot about a few things.

the highlight of the night was certainly me thinking i was calling tom's cell phone but actually dialing his house and yelling at his mother in a fake indian accent. oops.

Suffolk is officially Anti-War

By
Nick DeLena
on March 13, 2003 12:46 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

yesterday was one of the most remarkable days in my years at suffolk. the members of our suffolk university students for peace and justice movement submitted a resolution to the student government association that if adopted would mean that the student body of suffolk university officially condemns any military activity in iraq without u.n. authorization. it passed 8-7-1. it was incredible.

i have been extremely busy this semester. i'm barely at the apartment. i come home to sleep, then wake up and scurry back out.

as we pass through mid-term season, i feel more and more frightened of entering the real world and i have been tending to be more and more irresponsible. i'm at the bar almost every night, i'm spending more than i can afford to spend and i hang out with my friends instead of doing homework. maybe i'm just trying to hold on to what i have before time takes it away. i will miss writing for the paper.

as far as my personal life has been: a flurry of activity in recent weeks, but nothing substantial with which i emerge. such is life.

i'm hoping this thursday night will be a big one at the Hat. i need some drinking.

seattle washington 03

By
Nick DeLena
on March 9, 2003 12:51 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

the going joke around my friends is that my heart has been replaced with a circuit board. i upgraded to sarcasm, and maybe i'll buy empathy soon.

i just returned from a journalism conference in seattle put on by the associated college press. it was relatively amazing. it was three days of seminars and speaker events. notables were dan savage and gus van sant.

we stayed in the radisson, near rainier square. yes, it rained. we drank martinis in the hotel lounge with journalism wonks from all over the states.

the conference gave me some good ideas, stylistically, but i still feel like i have writers block in getting some of my opinions together on paper. i have a column to write by tomorrow night and i have to work tomorrow.

faces in disguise, not a trace of desire
