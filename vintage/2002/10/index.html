singing a song of boredom

By
Nick DeLena
on October 7, 2002 3:08 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i am sitting in the glorious mildred f. sawyer library with nothing to do for the next hour and a half. maybe i'll start my bullshit six page "autobiography" for my diversity class - you know, one of those classes that are required because the university has some kind of half-assed thirst for a well-balanced liberal education? yeah, i've got one of those classes. we sit around in a circle and talk about "what it means" to be white, or to be middle-class, or the son of a senator. i tire of such nonsense. put me back in accounting. or at least something interesting like ancient roman history.

there is a book immediately to my right: "american workingclass culture".

perhaps i'll give it a read.

question of the day

By
Nick DeLena
on October 2, 2002 11:04 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

can an overheating dsl modem cause a network slowdown?

i put my dsl modem underneath my router... my network has been lethargic lately. i just felt the modem and it was considerably hot. i have once again moved them apart. let's see if there is any difference in performance.

geek issue #1

By
Nick DeLena
on October 2, 2002 4:32 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

here's my problem:

since i host my own website, and don't use dyndns (or any other dns auto updaters) its availability is pretty much dependent on how often the isp changes my ip address. when i had at&t, this only happened every two or three months, but now that i am on verizon dsl, it happens at least once a week, maybe more, in addition to frequent (but momentary) outages.

since my father now has at&t broadband at home, wha
t i might do is take that old 850mhz system i have at home and turn it into a webserver, run a telnet and ftp server, and just deal with it remotely. this would work perfectly except for ip address changes. i need a program that checks for ip address changes and notifies me of the new address (or better yet, auto updates). wouldn't it be great if someone (james) wrote a little program for me? (yes, it would).

looks like friday's task is going to be to set up this server.
i'll do it quickly, so i can go back to beer drinking and talking about football and women.
