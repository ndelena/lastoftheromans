erg. l

yech. yech. drink drink drink.
feel woooozy.

building cities where highways intersect

By
Nick DeLena
on December 25, 2002 3:03 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i bought lottery tickets tonight for the first time since 1998.

'I had the shot, so I took it.'

'You took it- and broke a major rule of engagement.'

I don't particularly endorse the lottery as a surefire method of financial growth, but the prospect of a $280 million jackpot made my dissuasion a bit difficult.

earlier, at work, i was discussing with james what i would do with such immense wealth. my first step would be to secure and insure the money, then to buy my mother and father houses, wherever they wanted. i would then begin looking for a multi-level loft apartment. i think i would pick the corner of arlington & newbury as an ideal (and ultra swank) location. skylights are a must, and fireplaces in every room. on the first night in the apartment, i would owe it to myself to throw a massive, raging party. my twelve month goal would be to start a venture capital firm. and i would finish my degree, as scheduled, in may. but enough of that pipe dream.

tonight as i drove back from revere, i noticed that the roads were fairly congested. at first it shocked me, it was after midnight on christmas eve - shocked almost as if driving at this hour was illegal - and that we were all in quiet collusion - a rolling rebellion of solidarity to all the evils of christmas.

Requiem aeternam dona eis, Domine.
Et lux perpetua luceat eis.
Te decet hymnus, Deus, in Sion,
Et tibi reddetur votum in Jerusalem.
Exaudi orationem meam
Ad te omnis caro veniet.

there is a belief among christian fundamentalists that christ will return only when the jewish people have wholly reoccupied jerusalem. christ will return, and offer the jews a chance to convert to christianity. if they refuse, he will damn them to hell.

ad te omnis caro veniet indeed Domine.

while i am opposed to most theist machinations, i will offer a heartfelt wish for all:

Et lux perpetua luceat nos may perpetual light shine on us...

And when you've taken down your guard
If I could change your mind...
I'd really love to break your heart

the enamored blather of dead saints

By
Nick DeLena
on December 23, 2002 1:37 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

Zaterdag [dutch: Saturday] was a blur... 14, 15 beers? awake until 7am on the worn down wood of the living room floor playing 'drunk driver'. tom passed out. casey dropped out hours earlier.

i felt like mild death when i awoke five hours later. like the beer was seeping out my pores.

melissa told me of a flattering, if entirely ludicrous theory developed by the girls at 140 prince last weekend: that every girl i know is 'secretly in love' with me because i'm 'everything a girl wants'.

that's news to me.

this apparently followed a discussion on how talking to me is 'intimidating' to some people. [i think the example was a game of asshole we were playing where i asked a trivia question to give away a clear card and nobody could answer my questions. it wasn't like i was talking about quantum mechanics... i think i asked who was president before kennedy... daaaaaaaaaa. um, people, this is YOUR life.]

my goal is not to be intimidating, but to act as a catalyst for some kind of intellection. i realize that can be a lofty goal sometimes. but i will still cling to my romantic ideal of fraternization: sitting around a dimly lit table (preferably in europe) with a few friends, a few drinks, some cards, talking about politics, or literature, or film... but really talking about it, not just paying it lip service.

that's the stuff that gets me going.


*and now back to work*

instability abounds

By
Nick DeLena
on December 16, 2002 10:55 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

most exciting part of the day: my mp3 collection has swelled to 3,635.

today was one of those days where i wanted to destroy everything. i get that way sometimes.

on my way back from the gym in copley, on the 39, what appeared to be an unstable man started yelling at a man who was standing in his way saying "would you please move so i can get by?" and when it became apparent that the man had headphones on and didn't hear him, the guy says "oh, you didn't hear me because you have headphones on, you're one of those dumb fucks, you motherfucker, you motherfucker!"

as he was saying this, a woman who was sitting said "that's not polite language".

he instantly replied "shutup bitch, nobody told you to speak up."

unfortunately for me, my stop was next, and he also got off. and started talking to me.

"those people. those people on that bus. they are strange. they're so strange. those people from jamaica plain. they are so strange. i'm glad i don't live in jamaica plain. it's filled with lesbians, and... and feeeggeets and and and even hispanics!"

um, pal, you live on mission hill.

maybe the slaughter of the unstable isn't such a bad idea after all. imagine if this guy has kids.
