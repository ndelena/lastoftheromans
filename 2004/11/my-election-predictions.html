My Election Predictions

By
Nick DeLena
on November 1, 2004 10:12 PM | Permalink | Comments (23) | TrackBacks (0)

Following in the like of Tom's Patriots football predictions, I'm issuing my night-before prediction of the election. I'll follow it up on Wednesday with a post-election report card of how I did relative to the actual results. "Swing" states are in bold.

Alabama (9 votes): Bush by 15%
Alaska (3 votes): Bush by 27%
Arizona (10 votes): Bush by 14%
Arkansas (6 votes): Bush by 9%
California (55 votes): Kerry by 14%
Colorado (9 votes): Kerry by <1%

Florida (27 votes): Kerry by <1%
Georgia (15 votes): Bush by 13%
Hawaii (4 votes): Kerry by 8%
Idaho (4 votes): Bush by 27%
Illinois (21 votes): Kerry by 10%
Indiana (11 votes): Bush by 14%
Iowa (7 votes): Kerry by 4%
Kansas (6 votes): Bush by 22%
Kentucky (8 votes): Bush by 14%
Louisiana (9 votes): Bush by 9%
Maine (4 votes): Kerry by 7%
Maryland (10 votes): Kerry by 9%

Minnesota (10 votes): Kerry by 5%
Mississippi (6 votes): Bush by 10%
Missouri (11 votes): Bush by 3%
Montana (3 votes): Bush by 19%
Nebraska (5 votes): Bush by 28%

New Hampshire (4 votes): Kerry by 2%
New Jersey (15 votes): Kerry by 9%
New Mexico (5 votes): Kerry by 1%
New York (31 votes): Kerry by 18%
North Carolina (15 votes): Bush by 9%
North Dakota (3 votes): Bush by 20%

Rhode Island (4 votes): Kerry by 20%
South Carolina (8 votes): Bush by 16%
South Dakota (3 votes): Bush by 18%
Tennessee (11 votes): Bush by 17%
Texas (34 votes): Bush by 22%
Utah (5 votes): Bush by 39%
Vermont (3 votes): Kerry by 15%
Virginia (13 votes): Bush by 4%
Washington (11 votes): Kerry by 9%
West Virginia (5 votes): Bush by 8%
Wisconsin (10 votes): Kerry by 3%
Wyoming (3 votes): Bush by 33%

Washington DC (3 votes): Kerry by 65%

Total Electoral Vote tally: Kerry: 320, Bush: 218.

Kerry's margin of victory in the popular vote: 4%

I know this seems like a Kerry blowout, but I've gone over all the polls in the past week and they all show a very favorable trend for Kerry. And of course, if I'm wrong, we don't have to wait too long for that to become apparent.
