Labour pays the price

By
Nick DeLena
on October 7, 2004 1:44 PM | Permalink | Comments (9) | TrackBacks (0)

There was a remarkable by-election in English city of Hartlepool last week. It is remarkable in two ways: 1) Tony Blair's Labour nearly lost one of their strongholds 2) The Tories (Conservatives) have been pushed to 4th place (!) behind the Liberal Democrats, and the UK Independence Party.

In the election graph at left, you can see the current election result compared with the percentage change from the last election. What this means to me is that voters are increasingly looking for an alternative to Blair's Labour, and are looking to alternative parties, not the Tories for that change.

In more international elections worth watching, the first test since the Iraq war for Conservative Australian PM John Howard comes this Saturday as he seeks to hold his job for a fourth term against Labour's Mark Latham.
