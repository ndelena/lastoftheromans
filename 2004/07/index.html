Waxing Democratic

By
Nick DeLena
on July 29, 2004 2:21 PM | Permalink | Comments (3) | TrackBacks (0)

DNC week continues here in Boston. Everything by and large has gone smoothly; there have been little or no traffic snarls as was expected, protests have been largely sparse and peaceful and there is a tangible atmosphere of excitement and hope here.

Monday was one of the more exciting days, Brian and I were at a Young Democrats of America event at the Red Hat and got interviewed by several organizations, one of which was based in Switzerland. And, let us not forget the now infamous Boston.com appearance.

I've also been a regular at the MSNBC tent in Fanueil Hall. It's really one of the only publically accessible media tents around. Kudos to them. Chris Matthews is the man.

As you've all probably seen, the speeches have been relatively impressive, notably Barak Obama's and Bill Clinton's. Edwards' speech was enjoyable for style. I was a bit disappointed by Ted Kennedy's speech. But I suppose nothing will compare to his 1980 concession.

For an ongoing visual summary of my DNC week, check out http://lastoftheromans.org/gallery.

Liver damage up, bank accounts down.

We are our own proportionate example

By
Nick DeLena
on July 7, 2004 3:04 AM | Permalink | Comments (1) | TrackBacks (0)

You know, we (America) are our own proportionate example of ourselves... we are a socioeconomic superpower backwater - a small town grown world capitol. Most Americans maintain a small town-style ignorance in the face of increasingly internationalist involvement by our government.

Bill Cosby recently has come un
der fire for his criticism of the African-American community's position on its own position of education and advancemnt and so forth. I lament that there is no single force decrying America's own ignorance over its own foreign policy. It's come to a boiling point; no longer can we feign ignorance about the world - we're the inheritors of European Imperialism. Either we suck it up and recognize this, or we suck it up and do away with it. There can be no permanent inbetween.

Another example of media censorship

By
Nick DeLena
on July 5, 2004 7:52 PM | Permalink | Comments (1) | TrackBacks (0)

Michael Berg, father of murdered businessman Nick Berg is being consistently denied by U.S. media of expressing his opinion on his son's death. The elder Berg is an anti-war activist, but you wouldn't know it from American media, since they've barely quoted him since his son's death in early May. In yet another example of the dreadful homogeny of domestic news coverage, you can only find Berg's statments in the British press.

More here: http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/middle_east/3721207.stm
