The World Wants Kerry

By
Nick DeLena
on September 14, 2004 10:54 AM | Permalink | Comments (32) | TrackBacks (0)

In a new global poll done by Globescan (click for more info), a 5-to-1 majority of polled citizens across 35 countries would like to see John Kerry elected US President over George Bush. In this massive poll, 34,330 people were asked both who they would like to see elected and also how the Bush Presidency has affected their views of U.S. foreign policy. Out of the 35 nations, only 3 had a margin favoring Bush. A great plurality said they viewed U.S. foreign policy more negatively after four years of Bush.

Selected countries: (% for Kerry, %for Bush)


Norway: 74%-7%
Germany: 74%-10%
France: 64%-5%
Italy: 58%-14%
Spain: 45%-7%
UK: 47%-16%
Canada: 61%-16%
Mexico: 38%-18%
Brazil: 57%-14%
China: 52%-12%
Japan: 43%-32%
Indonesia: 57%-34%
India: 34%-33%

The five countries where Bush topped Kerry were: Philippines, Nigeria and Poland (India and Thailand were split-even).
