wie Regen auf einem Grab

By
Nick DeLena
on June 5, 2004 3:25 PM | Permalink | Comments (5) | TrackBacks (0)

Neglectful I have been, most neglectful. In starting this entry off, I'm reminded of the countless blogs you see across the net where every entry decries the lack of more frequent updates, leaving the site generally bereft of content. I don't want this to turn into one of those sites.

I'm back on Beacon Hill. I know, I'm all over the place. I've decided to move back until the lease expires. I never found a subletter, so I'm reoccupying once-native territory. Like Belisarius retaking Rome in 536: "The Rome that Belisarius entered reflected the general decline of the western empire. Though still the largest city in the west, its population had shrunk, people drove cattle through the forums, and buildings destroyed by the Visigoths and Vandals in the last century had not been repaired."

I reentered the apartment to find furniture unkempt and broken, cupboards raided and vacant, my room empty and dust-filled, and my roommate half-starved and xenophobic. But I come to integrate, not to conquer, just as Belisarius came at the behest of Justinian to rid the west of barbarians. An ambitious and ultimately unsuccessful expedition. Wie Regen auf einem Grab - "like rain on a grave."

I've been wholly occupied with coursework & web design of late.
