Supreme Court wakes up

By
Nick DeLena
on June 30, 2004 11:21 AM | Permalink | Comments (2) | TrackBacks (0)

It looks like the Supreme Court might have woken up and realized in order to preserve the checks and balances of the U.S. government, all three branches of the government must be active. In Hamdi v. Rumsfeld, decided June 28, 2004:

JUSTICE OíCONNOR, joined by THE CHIEF JUSTICE, JUSTICE KENNEDY, and JUSTICE BREYER, concluded that although Congress authorized the detention of combatants in the narrow circumstances alleged in this case, due process demands that a citizen held in the United States as an enemy combatant be given a meaningful opportunity to contest the factual basis for that detention before a neutral decisionmaker. Pp. 14ñ15. JUSTICE SOUTER, joined by JUSTICE GINSBURG, concluded that Hamdiís detention is unauthorized, but joined with the plurality to conclude that on remand Hamdi should have a meaningful opportunity to offer evidence that he is not an enemy combatant.

No surprise that Justice Scalia filed a dissenting opinion (which was probably written by Dick Cheney). From his opinion, it wasn't altogether clear what he was advocating...
