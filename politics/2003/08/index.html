A Dean & Clark ticket?

By
Nick DeLena
on August 26, 2003 1:07 PM | Permalink | Comments (2)

Last night while watching Hardball with Chris Matthews, there was a little blurb scrolling across the bottom that hinted at a rumor of retired General Wesley Clark joining Howard Dean as his runningmate for the '04 election. This could damn well be one of the better things I've heard in a while. I can safely say at this point I am behind Dean, but getting him elected in this country is another matter entirely. Having a retired General who was NATO Supreme Allied Commander Europe for several years might damn well bolster the case. This is good news indeed.

Dean, as of today, is the expected winner in the two most crucial primaries: New Hampshire and Iowa. I'm embarking on a quest to convince everyone I know to vote for Dean in the primary. It's going to be a bit of a challenge in Kerry territory here (the guy's condo is three streets over from me), but I think I can at least change a few people's votes by touting Dean's impressive credentials as Governor of VT.

To me this is an ideal matchup. Dean needs to appear more grounded to win and Clark, a career military man with a background in economics would surely make that ticket much more rounded and credible to a majority of Americans.

This could be the dose of good news that stokes my dormant optimism.

Perhaps it is due to the configuration of my non cable-ready television, but it seems like every time I watch TV I have to put up with seeing Ann Coulter's face on it. For those that don't know, she's (and please note the use of quotations here) a "conservative intellectual" (oxy moron..?). She wrote the books High Crimes and Misdemeanors, Slander and Treason: Liberal Treachery from the Cold War to the War on Terrorism. The latest of which alleges that anyone to the political left of a hardened Republican is a traitor; an America-hater, deserving of death.

Sounds like utter garbage, right? One would think. I've not seen any favorable reviews of her work, I've not read anything by her that I could agree with, and yet I must see her on television almost once a day. How is it that this woman
