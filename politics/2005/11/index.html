Quo Vadis, Revere?

By
Nick DeLena
on November 11, 2005 5:51 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

It was a tough week in municipal politics here. Brian Arrigo's bid for one of the five councilor-at-large seats fell short of the mark by 879 votes. It was a great campaign and I was privileged to be a part of it. We ran it cleanly and spread the message that there is a next generation of leadership in Revere - that there is hope for the city. I don't think you can find anything negative out there about Brian or about the campaign. Though citywide we were short of the mark, we had several bright spots across the city. Once again I can say with pride that I live in the Point of Pines - a neighborhood and precinct that is known for demanding more from its elected officials. My neighborhood gave Brian 188 votes - placing him in 4th. Only 2 votes behind Bob Haas, a former 4-term mayor. Another bright spot was Brian's childhood home precinct of Oak Island, he came in 2nd place, nearly topping political patriarch George Colella, finishing only 4 votes behind him.

In all, over 2,400 people out of 8,128 voters wanted Brian, a political newcomer, as their representative on the Revere City Council. I think that says a lot and it's something to be proud of and it carries a responsibility, as Ted Kennedy once said, "For all those whose cares have been our concern, the work goes on, the cause endures, the hope still lives, and the dream shall never die."

"We're a group. We're a team. From the President and Leo on through, we're a team. We win together, we lose together. We celebrate and we mourn together. And defeats are softened and victories are sweeter because we did them together... You're my guys and I'm yours... and there's nothing I wouldn't do for you." - Toby Zeigler on NBC's The West Wing.

The real world series

By
Nick DeLena
on November 4, 2005 5:43 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

In light of recent sports events, I thought I'd shed some light on the real world series:

- Bush buck-passing over Katrina
- House Majority Leader Tom DeLay indicted
- Indictment of "Scooter" Libby in CIA leak investigation
- Is Karl Rove next?
- Long reach of Abramoff investigation (David Safavian, Timothy Flanigan)
- All time low approval ratings (37% approve, 59% disapprove)
- Iraq quagmire
- Harriet Miers withdrawal (Republican v. Republican)
- Republican backlash over spending (Republican v. Republican)

"In my administration," Bush told voters in Pittsburgh in October 2000, "we will ask not only what is legal but what is right, not what the lawyers allow but what the public deserves."

Not quite.
