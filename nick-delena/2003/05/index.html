a chain of thoughts produced by an extended visit with family

By
Nick DeLena
on May 25, 2003 8:10 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

it is a strange thing becoming an adult. i've noticed the past few years that i'm seeing things in my family that i never have before. i am now preoccupied with the shortcomings of the relatives that i thought very highly of before. and those whom i had little in common i am finding had traits and interests all along that are not so dissimilar from where i am now.

i suppose it is a peculiar thing.

in other news work has begun to eat my life; i've now gone and made another financial committment to netModus, a web development side business i've started. i just purchased a very nice web server from macena systems. a custom built machine. the price was quite reasonable as well. i'm upgrading my internet service too, when i move to the apartment. in preparation for upcoming business activities.

so work is plenty, but the joys of an active and vibrant personal life are somewhat attenuated.

small time shot away

By
Nick DeLena
on May 24, 2003 11:24 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

after getting about three hours of sleep thursday night, i awoke yesterday surprisingly refreshed. for about ten minutes. i nearly fell asleep on the ride to work. but i got there. with a coffee.

yesterday was the first day in a good two weeks that my boss has been in the office. the second i walk in she throws 53 things at me. (annoyed face). but i cleared most of them up.

we were invited by the new business unit we joined to go to 53 state st for a lunch gathering. so i had some free pizza and they told me they were all going out to coogan's pub at 3:30 or so, since it was the day before the holiday. my boss thought it would be a good idea for me to attend, so i could rub elbows with the VPs of the group, as i am in desperate need of a full-time, salaried job.

needless to say it took very little convincing to get me to go to a bar on the company tab.

so i went and it was a really good time. i schmoozed. i think it may have quite positively affected my career objectives.

slate-based faction procurement

By
Nick DeLena
on May 19, 2003 12:04 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

dear world,

i write to you from my new office. we moved today from 245 summer to world trade west. somehow during the move, however, my computer decided to stop working. so i've been walking around, trying to entertain myself while several "techs" try and fix my computer. i attempted to take the shuttle back downtown but was paged by my boss to come back to the office for a conference call.

i drove to work this morning from revere. i took the new ted williams tunnel. i left my house at 7:45 and got to work at 8:10. that is such an unprecedented level of efficiency.

my new office is crazy. i'm going to take some pictures and post them. check back soon.

without you i'm still something

By
Nick DeLena
on May 11, 2003 4:02 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

when today started i was in a fairly miserable mood. maybe it was allergies, maybe fatigue. i felt entirely melancholy and bitter. but as the day progressed my mood improved. i just got back from a virtual spain reunion. most of the superstars i went to spain with all met up tonight downtown and we had many a cactus bowl at zuma's.

sometimes i find it hard to accept that i am an adult now; a grown man, with his own experiences and life to build and grow and nurture. but it's nights like these that make me realize and appreciate that fact. when friends from a wild and vivid adventure come around it makes me grateful to be me.

but sometimes i get bored of my own thoughts and wish back to a time when (seemingly) my thoughts were so new and unique and i truly felt like i was on the cusp of a new and great beginning in life. perhaps i was; and i'm on that path now. i guess i'm just hard to satisfy.

i suppose that's enough to think about for a drunken early morn.
