A strong and silent typeface

By
Nick DeLena
on November 29, 2003 9:00 PM | Permalink | Comments (4)

Wednesday evening I got a call from Joe C. about going to Reardon's in Revere for what is apparently a well-known "reunion" night for RHS grads. Normally, I would not have attended such an event, but I felt at this point it might be interesting to make an appearance to people I haven't seen in five years - most of whom I utterly detested.

I had a much better time than expected; within a few minutes I had ran into several old friends and we were taking shots. I ended up talking to several people whom I had written off many years ago as shallow or worthless. As is sometimes the case, I had not given these people a chance just as they had not given me one. I made plans with several of them to meet up at some point in the near future. It was as if I relived high school in four hours, erasing what ill memories I held. But, as Joe said, "I think everybody would have been nicer in high school if they were drunk."

A heartbreaker's heartbreaker

By
Nick DeLena
on November 29, 2003 5:53 PM | Permalink | Comments (2)

My previous weekend was spent in New York City with Jim and Tom. The initial plan for this trip developed before the ex and I had started speaking again, but after that point it more or less became a trip in honor of her 22nd birthday.

It is then rather strange that I did not see her for more than perhaps four hours over the course of the weekend. I must, however, respect circumstance.

"Can You Go Somewhere Else?"

I've cast aside my initial sense of guilt as she is involved with someone now. And things are jovial.

Feliz cumpleaños a Mi, bishis.

By
Nick DeLena
on November 18, 2003 10:51 AM | Permalink | Comments (0)

As you, my throng of varied admirers may well be aware, yesterday was my 23rd birthday. Apart from this small fact there is nothing additional to report. I am slightly older, as always.

This weekend we will be headed to New York City for a busy weekend of buses, museums, cafes, bookstores, subways, ethnic foods, bars, and parties. And the drive back.

Other news: Cetera magazine is online. Thanks to everyone involved for their hard work and contributions. If I get any free time we are going to focus on getting it out in print, as best we can with our limited resources.

And as always, if anyone is interested in contributing, please e-mail me.
