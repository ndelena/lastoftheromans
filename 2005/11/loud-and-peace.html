Loud and peace

By
Nick DeLena
on November 30, 2005 5:59 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

When I was in Rome in July, Kristen and I had noticed a phenomenon of sorts - masses of Europeans wearing shirts with English-language phrases. That in itself isn't remarkable, but what made it amusing to us was that the phrases made little or no sense and often were grammatically... creative. After the first day we started writing down ones we saw that we thought were most funny. I've sat on this list for quite a while and now that I have some free time I'm putting it up here for you all to read. Please note - that if you think you see a typo, chances are that is how it was spelled on the shirt.

With that, now, I give you Amusing English-language Phrases Found on European T-Shirts:

- "Why boys like me need boxe."
- "Fucker boys"
- "Back door boy"
- "Canterbury 8th Man"
- "Guess 8 Fabulous"
- "Fashion Sports"
- "Jeans is now more important than what they are."
- "Blujeans" (written on pink shirt)
- "Diesel O.T. Brave, O.I"
- "Clubs vs. Fighters"
- "Runway 2013"
- "This is the place."
- "Loud and peace"
- "I want more sex than my lover glam"
- "Love beautiful. No war! Are."
- "You! Insane Brain. Out of control!"
- "Looking your eyes, touching your skin, lobing you is a pleasure every day. Take me with you. Love and please." (written on a handbag)
- "Grim wide"
- "Fast track gas MC"
- "Soccer pure joy"
- "Ethnical fishes"
- "Deniro Spy News" (w/ NBC logo)
- "Fashion Londriver"
- "Cold Drinks Inside"
- "Internet Sexplorer 6.9" (with Internet Explorer logo and picture of Jesus)
- "Be Pinko"
- "Beach Youthful 8"
- "Sea Strong Girl Minority Club"
- "Surrealism Amplitude"
- "It's a short think"
- "Speedway Inc. Racers"
- "Friendly Club Sweet Years 50"
- "I am an Icon."
- "This is my most glamorous shirt."
