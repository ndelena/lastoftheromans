The April axiom

By
Nick DeLena
on April 11, 2004 10:25 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

There are a few new developments of late, nothing overwhelmingly significant. I purchased a '94 Volkswagen Jetta GLS a few weeks ago. It was purchased hit and is getting fixed as we speak. Quick stats: 5spd, 100k mi, sunroof, power windows/locks, Sony CDX-F7700 mp3/xm/cd player.

I have gotten new netModus business cards as I'm gearing up my web design business.

I won $60 playing poker Wednesday.

In yet another example of my notoriously mundane dreams (and speaking of business cards), last night I dreamt that all the business cards I keep stored in my organizer disappeared. That's it; that's the extent of my dream. The business cards were gone. End of dream. Is my imagination gone? Is that the best my subconscious can conjur? I hope it's because I'm just so damn creative when awake that sleep brings rest to my subconscious as well (blatant lies, blatant lies).

I spent some time with one of the exes this weekend. I had a great time, for good or ill. All my lovers were there with me, all my past and futures, and we all went to heaven in a little row boat. There was nothing to fear and nothing to doubt... If only things were so poetically ideal.


Old songs stay till the end. Sad songs remind me of friends. And the way it is, I could leave it all.
