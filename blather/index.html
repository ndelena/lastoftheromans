Following Jim's lead, I've installed TypeKey, a comment authorization service. I've been thinking about installing it for a long time to keep spam comments away, but the final straw was today, when I had to delete over 350 spam comments. For those of you unfamiliar with the movabletype blogging engine, it's not exactly fast. So that said, you've just got to register once (and you can use that same identity across all sites using TypeKey). Sorry for the inconvenience.

In other geeky concerns, does anyone out there use iTunes to rip cds? My concern is if the code contains information about ME. Any ideas?

wie Regen auf einem Grab

By
Nick DeLena
on June 5, 2004 3:25 PM | Permalink | Comments (5) | TrackBacks (0)

Neglectful I have been, most neglectful. In starting this entry off, I'm reminded of the countless blogs you see across the net where every entry decries the lack of more frequent updates, leaving the site generally bereft of content. I don't want this to turn into one of those sites.

I'm back on Beacon Hill. I know, I'm all over the place. I've decided to move back until the lease expires. I never found a subletter, so I'm reoccupying once-native territory. Like Belisarius retaking Rome in 536: "The Rome that Belisarius entered reflected the general decline of the western empire. Though still the largest city in the west, its population had shrunk, people drove cattle through the forums, and buildings destroyed by the Visigoths and Vandals in the last century had not been repaired."

I reentered the apartment to find furniture unkempt and broken, cupboards raided and vacant, my room empty and dust-filled, and my roommate half-starved and xenophobic. But I come to integrate, not to conquer, just as Belisarius came at the behest of Justinian to rid the west of barbarians. An ambitious and ultimately unsuccessful expedition. Wie Regen auf einem Grab - "like rain on a grave."

I've been wholly occupied with coursework & web design of late.

There are a few new developments of late, nothing overwhelmingly significant. I purchased a '94 Volkswagen Jetta GLS a few weeks ago. It was purchased hit and is getting fixed as we speak. Quick stats: 5spd, 100k mi, sunroof, power windows/locks, Sony CDX-F7700 mp3/xm/cd player.

I spent some time with one of the exes this weekend. I had a great time, for good or ill. All my lovers were there with me, all my past and futures, and we all went to heaven in a little row boat. There was nothing to fear and nothing to doubt... If only things were so poetically ideal.

The Common inheritance

By
Nick DeLena
on March 16, 2004 5:55 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

I spent the past weekend in New York City generally loafing and sipping exorbitantly priced vodka tonics. I spent most of Saturday hanging out with Phaea, having lunch in Brooklyn at 2nd Street Cafe in Park Slope (go there, it was really good).

>"Being alive is a lonely thing."

We hit up some galleries in SoHo, caught a movie (Wilbur Wants to Kill Himself) at the Angelika, a burrito at Chipotle on St. Marks, and some drinks at Bull McCabe's and Stingy Lulu's.

I had some strange dreams that night. One involved me throwing new CDs down a ski slope, in another I converted a large recreational vehicle into a massive bathtub, and in yet another I was at my old job listening to a former co-worker describe a girl as "professionally soft and shiny." (I'm thinking that sleeping in the bed of an ex-girlfriend led to some unusually bizarre brain activity.)

Sunday morning saw a handsome poached egg mechanism on white toast at Park Slope Restaurant and me leaving Phaea and taking the F to 2nd Ave to check into my cheapo place on Bowery St (The Whitehouse Hotel). I had a single room, roughly seven times larger than a shoebox and with strange and exposed ceilings (perhaps to keep people from suffocating?). But all for $27. I met up with the Perpetually Plastered club for the day, spent mostly around the lower East side between Canal and East 18th. I got to meet Miranda's parents, who were very nice. We had dinner at Benny's Burritos and drinks later on at Solas on East 9th. Jon came in for dinner with us and hung out for some drinks. Later on Jon and I walked (read: stumbled) to get some pizza and spaz around the city for a while. Then I passed out.

I took the Metro North back to Westport, CT around 11am, getting to my car at around 12. My return to Revere was less than exciting.

Every time I go to NYC it merely reconfirms my desire to live there. With any luck I'll be down there by September or January.

What can be said?

By
Nick DeLena
on January 28, 2004 6:02 PM | Permalink | Comments (4)

To those that have aptly noticed, my server has been down for some 22 days. It has not been an easy 22 days I assure you. Probably some of the worst ever.

This year started off on a high note and overconfidently (and despite the quite negative parking bane) I strode into it with vigor. Then I was laid off my job at Fidelity after working there five years. An unfortunate consequence of that act is my having to sublet my room at 9Grove. Back to Revere I have come. Humbled, yes, but perhaps with a better platform here to come back from. The tennants on the first floor of my father's house have long moved out, and the apartment remained unoccupied, so it is into this space that I've moved. So I have a two bedroom apartment (roughly 1200 sq feet) to myself for the time being. I've been getting it set for a remodeling - the walls are in need of paint desperately, and the painting is set to start Saturday. New furniture is coming in.

If that were the limits to the ill-fate that has befallen me, it would be bad enough. But I have the fortune of having some disheartening things happen of a more personal nature that have left me puzzled.

And as I stop to write all this it is a wonder I am not more downtrodden than I already am. Just another brick in the wall I guess.
