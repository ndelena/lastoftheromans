Job-Related: December 2002 Archives

long day

By
Nick DeLena
on December 30, 2002 10:49 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

i hate mondays.

once again i could be found among the corporate trappings of fidelitor investmonger. i worked about nine hours today, then headed back to 1625! before going to the gym in copley. i rocked about 530 calories off. i'm not even tired now. i almost just broke out the espresso cooker and made my trademark, servicemark, copyrighted espresso & french vanilla cream but i decided against it, as tomorrow, i once again must raise myself before the cold-fearing sun peaks its head above the dirty, snowy streets of this fair city.

"You're working on new year's eve?"

Hey, if the stock market is open, I'm open baby.

i have officially been indoctrinated into the corporate/IT realm with the arrival of my two-way skytel pager. if you've ever walked down the financial district, it seems like at least a quarter of the peole walking around have these things clipped to their sides. anyway this thing is pretty cool. it is its own e-mail box- you can dial a 1-800 number and enter my PIN and have a message sent, you can send an e-mail directly to it, or you can send a message through the skytel website. and it has a little keyboard on it so i can write back.

but the best part: i can get an unlimited amount of news, weather, market updates. right now i have market updates at 9, 1, and 4:30pm. so i get market predictions at 9, a mid-day update at 1 and a closing analysis at 4:30. that and weather and us/world news updates throughout the day. how do they expect me to get more work done now? ha ha.

geekyproblems

By
Nick DeLena
on December 24, 2002 9:39 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

Looks like my web server disappeared. The IP address probably changed again. I think I'm going to install cygwin on there again and get that IP check program up and running, probably with James's help. Since Fidelity is getting me a Motorola T900 two-way pager, I can have the server page me when the address changes. That would be insane. I'm really at the point now where I might just go and get a speakeasy sdsl or adsl connection (with two static IPs) at the apartment. I could run the server there and never have to worry about IP address changes. The only problem is that the service is something on the order of $89/mo. Yikes. There is, perhaps, revenue potential there...

Anyway. It's about 10am, christmas eve. I drove to work today. It was a fairly astonishing process. I had resigned myself to a belief that Huntington Ave would be congested, and Stuart street a nightmare. I think I got here in 15 minutes. It was pretty amazing. The only negative: I'll probably pay about $15 in parking. Assholes.

This whole holiday season is bogus. It really is less about giving and caring than it is about revenue forecasts, eps goals, and quarterly reports. Don't tell me that earnings are flat - did you see the mall yesterday? Christ. These retail chains have to streamline if they can't make enough money from thirty days of mad, frantic shoppers filling the aisles of their stores.

Rock.


I'm leaving work at 1 today. I'm going to head to Revere and fix the stupid server. Then maybe go back to the apartment for a while and finally update my website (picture galleries, suffolk journal articles). I'm looking for ways to integrate or streamline some processes on there through ColdFusion, but I can't think of any cases where the functionality achieved would truly outweigh the amount of work done.

corporate gripe

By
Nick DeLena
on December 19, 2002 10:43 AM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)

There's a group here at Fidelity that developed a huge database for us, with a front-end interface that allows us to maintain a knowledgebase for this initiative we're working on. Even though it's the same company, they still bill us for the project. It seems stupid, I know. But what is stupid is that they bill us for the time it takes them to fix the bugs they created.

Maybe that can fly when it's an internal thing, but imagine if companies operated like this to each other. Imagine selling a product to someone broken and saying "wait, wait, if you want it to actually work, well then, that's another $200."
