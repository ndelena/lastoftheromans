Plus ça change?

By
Nick DeLena
on August 30, 2007 5:06 PM | Permalink | Comments (0) | TrackBacks (0)
Aesthe.nu is back online as Last of the Romans.org. It's been a long time coming (a year and a half?) but now the wheels are turning again.

Quick summary of the past year and a half: Republicans trounced in national elections, congressional and senatorial majorities wiped out, but I'm not entirely impressed with the new Democratic leadership either. Harry Reid has as much charisma as an elderly woman complaining about the price of soup. It's nice to see both a woman and an Italian-American in a position of leadership in the House of Representatives in Nancy Pelosi. She hasn't shot her own foot off either yet, which is encouraging.

The housing market seems a bit like the homeless man that cans at the corner of North Washington and Causeway streets in Boston. He has the ability to sleep standing up, while holding a sign, but sometimes slowly tips over to the point where you're not sure if he's going to fall or not. This market seems to be in a similar position, teetering on the brink of utter deflation, especially considering the amount of foreclosures that are going to be putting homes on the market (and people out of homes)... not good for those relying on equity or planning to sell.

Some of this could have been prevented by increased regulation in the lending industry to eliminate the predatory lending practices that have nearly bankrupted 2 million Americans.

In local developments: Is the renaissance of Revere at hand? Two major projects will be breaking ground over the next year along the Boulevard, the <a href="http://www.liveatoceanclub.com>Ocean Club</a> and the Waterfront Square project to develop Parcel H near Wonderland T station. Both are very aggressive by Revere standards: Ocean Club would place a massive development of upscale lofts along an undeveloped and overgrown stretch of the beach, including a pool and a massive amenity area (for residents). The nicest units are selling for over $1 million. Waterfront Square is a very aggressive mixed-use proposal that would sit on 10 acres next to Wonderland station (the northern terminus of the blue line subway) including a 13-story hotel, 902 condominiums, and 200,000 square feet of office, restaurant and retail space. It was written up by the Boston Globe today here. Maybe things are looking up.
