Fambrosium

By
Nick DeLena
on September 25, 2003 1:32 PM | Permalink | Comments (4)

As some of you may be aware, I recently found out that I am eligible for dual U.S.-Italian citizenship, through my great grandfather who was born in Italy. There is a clause where if your paternal great grandfather was an Italian citizen and your grandfather was born in the United States, your grandfather, father and yourself are eligible for dual citizenship. So I've started assembling the necessary paperwork. I found my great grandfather's immigration record at Ellis Island's website.

Name: DiLena, Gennara

Ethnicity: Italy, Central Italian

Residence: Afragola, Campania

Date of Arrival: June 15, 1905

Age on Arrival: 18y

Gender: M

Marital Status: S

Ship of Travel: The Roma

Port of Departure: Naples, Campania, Italy

Some time after his arrival, someone in the family decided to change their spelling of our last name to DeLena from DiLena. I was always under the impression that it was the other way around - that we were keeping the true spelling of the name - and now through a little digging I see that it was the other way around.

Next weekend I'll be taking a trip to the National Archives in Waltham to get more data.

On getting older... lux nascens.

By
Nick DeLena
on September 16, 2003 12:11 PM | Permalink | Comments (2)

Last Thursday was a particulary poignant moment of epiphany for me. After spending a few hours at the Red Hat, it soon became apparent and painfully obvious that we were surrounded by the vanguard of a nascent generation of Suffucks.

My reaction may have been a bit magniloquent; I waxed apocalyptic about my own obsolescence and withered in the thought that I was no more relevant to the people surrounding me than the pantheon to modern Romans - nothing more than a false axiom, with each successive era falling more and more into quiet, mythic sleep. Of what value are ruins?

It is apparent to see that I have not yet adjusted to the post-graduate existance.

I did what I could to cope; gorged myself on takeout from the Shang.

(Note to self: Never, NEVER order from the Shang again. And mean it this time.)

ad Elysium non ego vedere

By
Nick DeLena
on September 12, 2003 2:55 PM | Permalink | Comments (2)

Te credisne deum, qui captus carcere divo?
(Do you think yourself a god because you are held in a divine jail?)

That sums up quite nicely what I'm feeling at the moment. I finally am getting paid with my raise in effect. It is quite nice. I bought about $150 worth of books the other day. "A Long Short War" by Christopher Hitchens, two books on learning Latin, "Following Hadrian" - about Emperor Hadrian's 2nd century travels through the Roman Empire, a hardcover version of "Meditations" by Marcus Aurelius, and a huge photography collection of Roman ruins. Notice a theme here?

I might be on the eve of a sizable opportunity at work. Rumors abound that a new position is being created, a Chief Information Security Officer position, and our VP is slated to get the job - and wants my boss and I as part of his staff. So I would be much closer to the top and thus have an opportunity to be noticed. I am pretty excited. Although I still harbor the desire to write. But who would
pay to read my stuff? That is the fundamental question.

To Be Indexed

By
Nick DeLena
on September 2, 2003 11:33 AM | Permalink | Comments (7)

Last night I confessed my >brilliant< idea for a short film and was subsequently derided for being "too serious" when I'm out with friends. I have my share of serious moments and moments of absurd, self-deprecating jocularity, but I like to think I have struck a harmonious balance. Apparently there are people who disagree with that belief.

Jim and I were also told that we are somewhat intimidating to talk to; that we appear to deliberately condescend to certain people. I've gotten this before. I could apologize for the way I speak and how I behave, however, if I'm not doing so with any malevolent and condescending intent and the action is merely representative of my personality and instinctive behavior - then I don't believe any apologies or rectification is necessary. I won't apologize for who I am, etc.

Established: like post-rock, Portuguese wine, and the movie Waterworld, most people do not appreciate Nick DeLena.
