Edward Gibbon: You Are The Man.

By
Nick DeLena
on July 30, 2003 12:39 PM | Permalink | Comments (3)

I'm still ploughing through History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire. This man (Edward Gibbon) amazes me. He wrote a seven volume set. Some 3,800 pages over 10 years detailing every significant development of the Roman Empire from 180A.D. to 1453 (the fall of Constantinople). The language is beautiful to boot.

This past weekend I went down to Barnstable on the Cape for a clambake. I think that's what they call it anyway. They had this big fire and they put seaweed over it and then placed the food on top of that to cook. Apparently this is commonly known as the clambake cooking method. You'd figure that myself, being from Massachusetts, should know this. I did not know this. I guess I'm too much of a city dweller.

Surely the best part of the afternoon was when I was accosted by a portly girl for wearing 'pleated' shorts. I really can't recall our conversation (read: heated argument) that well, but she basically accosted me. I don't think I spoke to her at all before she proverbially sank my Arizona. It was rather strange. In the course of her criticism of my shorts, it somehow came to be revealed that she is a conservative Republican. I'm not sure what or how this related to my shorts, but it came to pass. (Perhaps she overheard me talking politics?) I don't know.

I do know, however, that at a point many minutes into our arguing I, somewhat intoxicatedly, told her that I'd burn her face. I meant it, then.

"Everybody has fascist tendencies."

A friend of mine told me that once and I find it to be frighteningly true. There are times I get so aggravated with people (and large groups of people) that I wish deep down that I had the means to force MY ideology on them. I know that is terribly fascist... and I know it's wrong. But sometimes my dark side rises dangerously close to the surface.

I'd make a really strange fascist dictator though. I guess I'd be more like a Soviet sort than anything else. I don't know... could their ever be a dictatorship that forces atheism, social tolerance, anti-militarism, education, and world peace? Sounds quite contradictory.

That's probably why after debating this in my head for hours I just give up and drink more.

There was another time that evening where a friend of the portly girl, this burly guy from Assfuck, New Hampshire with a scraggly beard wearing a basketball shirt chimed in to accost my sense of fashion as well. Isn't that irony? Anyway this chap and I had some words to exchange afterwards. I told Kevin about it later and he and a friend of his (in their exceedingly intoxicated state) stood behind him with matches and lighter fluid waiting for me to give the word. Imagine!

Maybe I'm not such a fascist after all. That would have been a turning point.

Oh Humanity. I can safely say that I didn't make any friends among this girl and her little clique. She got me so riled in the course of the argument that I was considerably en fuego and derided pretty much everything about her, about where she was from (Indiana), about her beliefs, her appearance, her voice, etc. Then she apologized. It was exceptionally bizarre. And tiring.
